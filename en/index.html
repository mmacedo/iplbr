Legislative Power Index

About

What is the Legislative Power Index?

The Legislative Power Index is an attempt to determine what is the power of each political party.

How is it calculated?

Analysing the election results and calculating the population represented by each political party, or more specifically, the population affected by legislation passed by the elected representatives of the party.

Brazil

The Brazilian state is a federative republic with three levels of government and three branches.

Three branches

In Brazil, the three branches of the state are:

  • The legislature is the branch tasked with enacting legislation;
  • The executive in the rule of law has no independent power to pass legislation, but has a limited power to affect its approval by the veto.
  • The judiciary is in theory impartial and non-partisan, so it is not used in the index.

To pass a bill it is necessary a simple majority, i.e., 50% + 1 of the present as long as at least 50% + 1 of all members are present (quorum). But a project can be vetoed by the Executive, then it is returned to the legislature that has the opportunity to override the veto. To override the veto and pass a bill that was not blessed with a sanction from the executive, it is necesary an absolute majority, i.e., 50% + 1 of all members of the house (not just the present).

No veto
Quorum=All×50%+1Simple Majority (min.)=Quorum×50%+1Simple Majority (max.)=All×50%+1

E.g. The Chamber of Deputies has 513 members:

Quorum=513×50%+1=257Simple Majority (min.)=257×50%+1=129≈25%Simple Majority (max.)=257×50%+1=257≈50%
With a veto
Absolute Majority=All×50%+1

E.g. The Chamber of Deputies has 513 members:

Absolute Majority=513×50%+1=257≈50%

If 50% of the members of the house are present (the minimum required for to vote a bill), it takes 50% of the votes with the sanction from the executive and 100% of the votes without it. If 100% of the members of the house are present, it takes 50% of the votes with or without the sanction from the executive. That is, depending on how many are present, the sanction of the executive can raise the vote requirement zero or up to 50%. To account for this, the executive is given a weight of 25% in the index (approximation of the mean between the two extremes):

Total=Legislature×0.75+Executive×0.25

Levels of government

In Brazil, there are three levels of government:

  • The federal level, also known in Brazil as the Union, comprises all of the Brazilian territory;
  • The state level, which comprises the 26 states and the Federal District;
  • The local level, that comprises all the municipalities of Brazil, except for Brasilia where the Federal District government accumulates the functions of its only municipality.

The index gives equal weight to each level of government:

Total=Federal+State+Local3
Federal Level

At the federal level, the executive functions are performed by the President of the Republic and the legislative functions by the National Congress, which is composed of two houses (or chambers), the Federal Senate and the Chamber of Deputies. All votes on bills and vetoes must pass in both houses.

Chamber=Federal DeputiesTotal of Federal DeputiesSenate=SenatorsTotal of SenatorsLegislature=Chamber+Senate2
Executive=Presidents1
State level

At the state level, the executive functions are performed by the governors and the legislative functions are performed by the unicameral legislative assemblies.

Legislature=State DeputiesTotal of State Deputies
Executive=GovernorsTotal of Governors
Local level

At the local level, the executive functions are performed by the mayor and the legislative functions are performed by the city councils.

Legislature=CouncilmembersTotal of Councilmembers
Executive=MayorsTotal of Mayors
Federal District

The Federal District is a Federative Unit like the states, but with a few differences:

  • While there are several urban areas tht could be described as cities, the Brazilian constitution stipulates that the national capital, Brasilia, is the single and indivisable municipality of the Federal District;
  • The city of Brasilia does not have its own administration. All the functions normally assigned to municipalities are responsibility of respective powers of the Federal District.

Thus, the same district deputy counts in the index as the equivalent of a state deputy for the Federal District and a councilmember for Brasilia.

Entities of the federation

Regardless of which state elected a federal deputy or senator, the bills on which they vote or that they write will be valid throughout Brazil, so the federal index of a Federative Unit or municipality is the same for all of them, just as the state index is the same for all municipalities of the same federative unit.

To calculate the state or local index for the country, it is necessary to combine all the indices of all states and municipalities, respectively. There are several ways to achieve this:

  • The nominal (unweighted) method is one in which all the representatives have equal power, for instance, all councilmembers of the country have the same weight regardless of which city they represent. The problem with this method is that while the number of mayors in a city is fixed to 1, the number of councilmembers varies. While the mayor of São Paulo would have a weight of (1 / 5,566) × 0.25, the entire city council of São Paulo would have a weight of (55 / 57,239) × 0.75 or approximately (1 / 1,040.71) × 0.75.
  • The method proportional to the legislature is equal to the nominal for the members of the legislature, therefore the mayor of São Paulo would have a weight of (55 / 57,239) × 0.25 just as the city council of São Paulo. We normalized the weight of the entity of the federation across all branches of government, but the number of councilmembers is still an imperfect measure of the relative weight of each entity in the federation. In 2012, the city of São Paulo, with a population of 11,376,685 had a weight of 1 / 1,040, and Blumenau, with a population of 316,139 had a weight of 1 / 3,816 or 27.3% of the weight of São Paulo with just 2.8% of its population.
  • The method proportional to the represented population, the Electoral Unit is given a weight proportional to the last population estimate given by the IBGE before that date. In 2012, the city of São Paulo would have a weight of 11,376,685 / 193,917,083 or 1 / 17.05 and Blumenau would have a weight of 316,139 / 193,917,083 ou 1 / 613.39. This was the method chosen for the calculation of this index.

Common Issues

Why the data starts in the 1982 elections?

These are the first elections after the Amnesty Law and the return of the multiparty system, although the municipalities, the territories, the Federal District, the newly created state of Rondônia and Federal Senate were still composed of non-elected representatives:

  1. For the first time, 201 municipalities were able to choose their mayors, including the state capitals.
  2. The Rondônia state and the Federal District elected their representatives for the first time.
  3. The bionic senators chosen in 1978 were replaced by elected ones.
  4. For the first time all municipalities were able to choose their representatives.
  5. The federal territories of Amapá and Roraima were elevated to state status by the Constitution of 1988 and thus they were able to choose their representatives for the first time. Tocantins also had their first election, but it was emancipated from Goiás, which already elected their representatives.

Why the total index is calculated starting in 1996?

It is the first election for which there are election data for all the representatives serving mandate (first data for local elections).

Why there is no data for the local elections of 1985, 1988 and 1992?

The elections of 1985 happened only in a few cities. There is no data on the elections of 1988 and 1992 in the TSE's website.

Why party X is coloured with colour X instead of colour Y?

There are several difficulties in using the colours of the parties: the parties have multiple colours, the colours do not look well in the chart, the colours repeat a lot across parties, etc. Therefore, colours are chosen in a more or less arbitrary manner to facilitate the comprehension of the chart. In most cases, the colours are shades of one the party colours.

Why the federal index for the place X is not proportional to the representatives elected by X?

The representatives in the federal level have legislative powers that affect the entire territory, not just the federative unit for which they were elected.

Why the number of representatives of party X is wrong in the date X?

The index does not track changes of affiliation, leaves, resignations, impeachments, suspensions, complementary elections, etc. The number of representatives of each party is based exclusively on the number representatives elected in nationwide elections.

Why is there data for local elections in the Federal District?

The representatives of the Federal District accumulate the functions of local and state governments. The local index for the Federal District is the same as the state index.

Why the sum of some indices do not equal 100%?

There are several problems that difficult the obtention of a more precise result:

  • The sum of the population estimates given by the IBGE do not always equal exactly 100% because of court decisions changing the estimate for any given municipality;
  • Some newly emancipated municipalities had elections before the IBGE had published a population estimate for them, so an estimate from the following year had to be used. So, a group of people may have been counted twice as part of two municipalities or not at all, generating significant disparities. Even if the new municipalities were formed only by people who did not exist in the previous estimate, the population changes from a year to another naturally or by migration, so when we use estimates from different years they are bound to have slight variations.
  • The database containing the results of the local elections from the TSE has many holes. There is no way of knowing if one or more representatives are missing and the index will be calculated proportionally to the number available, but if all representatives of a public post for a given place are missing, the sum of the indices for all parties will be zero, leaving a hole in the data proportional to the weight of the place.
